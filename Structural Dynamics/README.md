# Structural Dynamics

## What I Studied

**Writing the equations of motion**
- Discretizing continuous structures: lumped mass, shape functions, FEM
- Building mass and stiffness matrices from energy principles (Lagrange equations)
- Adding damping: viscous model, Rayleigh damping
- Seismic base excitation

**Solving for a single degree of freedom (SDOF)**
- Free vibration and effect of damping ratio ζ
- Harmonic loading: resonance, magnification factor, phase
- Arbitrary loading: Duhamel's integral and numerical time-stepping methods
- Frequency domain: FFT, transfer function, response spectrum

**Multi degree of freedom (MDOF)**
- Natural frequencies and mode shapes from the eigenvalue problem
- Modal superposition — decoupling the equations using principal coordinates
- Damped systems: Rayleigh damping, direct integration

**Continuous beams**
- Equation of motion as a PDE, solved by separation of variables
- Approximate methods: Galerkin, Rayleigh-Ritz
